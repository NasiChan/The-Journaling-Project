About The Journaling Project

The Journaling Project is your personal space to vent, reflect, and release. It's a place where you're encouraged to write about what's weighing you down—your frustrations, fears, heartbreaks, or anything that's on your mind.

Journaling isn't just a creative outlet—it's a powerful mental health tool. Instead of stacking emotions on top of each other until they explode, this project offers a gentle, private way to express them one by one.

Whether you're glowing with joy or spiraling in silence, The Journaling Project holds space for it all. It's especially helpful before therapy sessions, where it can help you organize your thoughts or bring up things you've been hesitant to say out loud.

This project was created by Nas—a curious coder and chronic overthinker—who believes that writing is a soft rebellion and that journaling heals.

If you have feedback or just want to say hi, feel free to reach out!

How to Use This in Therapy

The Burn Book can be your quiet preparation space before a therapy session. Here are a few ways it can help:

  • Note Patterns: Track recurring emotions or thoughts to understand what keeps coming up for you.
  • Write Freely: Use it to vent about things you're not ready to say out loud yet. Sometimes, writing it down first makes it easier to speak later.
  • Use Entries as Prompts: Bring your entries to therapy as starting points for deeper conversations.
  • Highlight Shifts: Notice how your tone or focus changes over time—it can reflect your growth.

There's no “right” way to use this. What matters is that it's honest, personal, and yours.
